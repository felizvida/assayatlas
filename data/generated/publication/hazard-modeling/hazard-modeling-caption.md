Figure 16. Clinical Relapse Hazard Modeling. Move from survival curves to interpretable hazard ratios across clinically meaningful covariates. The Cox model ranks prior convictions, age, and parole-related covariates as the strongest hazard modifiers, giving investigators a prioritized view of relapse risk rather than a single average survival curve. The statistical trick is the proportional-hazards model itself, which turns time-to-event data into interpretable hazard ratios while preserving follow-up information. Source data: rossi from the lifelines example datasets, framed here as a clinical relapse-risk modeling proxy.
