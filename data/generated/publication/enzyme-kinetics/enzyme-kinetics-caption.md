Figure 12. Enzyme Inhibition Kinetics Panel. Fit Michaelis-Menten curves for treated and untreated enzyme conditions. The treated and untreated curves separate in a way that suggests the intervention is changing catalytic behavior, not merely adding noise to the assay. Nonlinear Michaelis-Menten fitting is the crucial analytic step because it estimates Vmax and Km from the full curve shape rather than from a single substrate concentration. Source data: Puromycin from the R datasets collection, already close to a classic enzyme-kinetics assay example.
