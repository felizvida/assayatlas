Figure 18. Exposure-Associated Cancer Risk Model. Model case-control risk across exposure bins and show interpretable odds ratios. The model points to alcohol and tobacco exposure bins with materially elevated odds of case status, which is the biological question investigators actually care about in exposure-associated cancer risk work. Logistic regression is doing the heavy lifting by converting grouped case-control counts into adjusted odds ratios and confidence intervals instead of leaving you with raw percentages alone. Source data: esoph from the R datasets collection, already a biomedical case-control dataset.
