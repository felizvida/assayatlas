Figure 11. Cell-State Distribution Shift. Compare full distributions, not just means, across three biological states. The biological states differ not only in mean sepal length but also in spread and distribution shape, which is exactly why the violin view is more informative than a bare bar chart. The one-way ANOVA formalizes the omnibus mean-difference question (F 119.26, p < 0.001), while the violin geometry keeps heterogeneity visible. Source data: iris from the R datasets collection, framed here as a cell-state size-distribution proxy.
